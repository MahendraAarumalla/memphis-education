🤖 AI Analysis — Key Finding

From 2019 to 2024, Tennessee's statewide TCAP results look like a recovery story. But when districts are grouped by county median household income, a different pattern emerges. Math proficiency fell in 3 of 4 income bands — declining most sharply in the lowest-income quartile (−2.0 points avg) while only upper-middle-income districts held a marginal gain (+0.6). ELA improved across all groups, but low-income districts gained just 2.2 points vs. 4.6 points in lower-middle-income districts — barely half the recovery rate. Social Studies showed the widest divergence: upper-middle districts gained 6.4 points; low-income districts gained only 2.8. Memphis-Shelby County Schools — despite sitting in Shelby County's higher-income band — saw math proficiency drop 7.2 points (27.2% → 20.0%), the steepest decline of any large district in the state. Policy responses focused on statewide averages risk masking the communities where students are falling furthest behind.
