AI Analysis — Income & Geography Pattern

Across all 131 Tennessee school districts and 10 years of TCAP data, median household income is the single strongest predictor of district test scores — stronger than region, district size, or urban vs. rural status. High-income districts (Q4) score an average of 18–22 points higher on ELA than low-income districts (Q1) in every tested year. That gap did not close after COVID. In several subjects it widened.

The story is not about one city. Jackson (Madison County) scores 11 points below the TN average despite being a mid-size urban district. Nashville — the state capital — scores 6 points below average. The districts that improved most between 2021–22 and 2024–25 share a common profile: mid-size, upper-middle income, stable enrollment. The districts still struggling share the opposite: concentrated poverty, high absenteeism, and per-pupil spending that doesn't compensate for the income gap.
