Transportation & Family Burden
If your high school student is the primary driver in a family, that means they have to get their siblings to school on time and then show up to their class, which means they're not even in class until maybe third period.

Data Sources & Methodology
Source Tennessee Department of Education — District-Level Chronic Absenteeism Files, 2016-17 through 2024-25
URL tn.gov/education/districts/.../data-downloads.html ↗
Definition A student is chronically absent if they miss 10% or more of instructional days in a school year — approximately 18 days out of 180.
Filters Applied All Students subgroup · All Grades · District-level aggregate (not school-level)
Processing Raw files parsed with Python/pandas. Numbers verified against TDOE published reports.
⚠ 2017-18 data note: The MSCS chronic absenteeism figure for 2017-18 (12.9%) falls anomalously below adjacent years and even below the state average. This likely reflects data suppression or a methodology transition in TDOE reporting. Treat this data point with caution in trend analysis.
