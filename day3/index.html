🤖 AI Analysis — Key Finding

Across 124 Tennessee districts in 2023–24, absenteeism alone does not predict graduation rate — and the exceptions reveal the real story. Of 66 districts with above-average absenteeism, 26 still maintained above-average graduation rates, defying the expected pattern. Sequatchie County leads: 32.7% absenteeism yet 91.4% graduation. Meanwhile, Memphis-Shelby County Schools sits in the at-risk quadrant — 29.5% absenteeism and only 82.8% graduation, 7 points below the state average. The ACT composite gap is stark: Williamson County scores 25.2 while MSCS scores 16.4 — an 8.8-point gap within the same state funding system. Policy choices at the district level, not absenteeism alone, appear to determine whether students graduate.
