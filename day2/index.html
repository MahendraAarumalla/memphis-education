🤖 AI Analysis — Key Finding

Memphis City Schools' ACT composite score has declined every single year since 2019–20, dropping from 17.3 to 16.4 — a loss of nearly one full point. Despite testing 465 more students in 2024–25 than in 2019–20, the college-readiness rate fell from 19.2% to 15.8%. Today, 3 in 4 MSCS students score below the ACT 19 college-readiness benchmark. The sharpest drop came in 2020–21 — the first post-COVID year — and scores have never recovered. Math and English saw the steepest declines; Science is the only subject showing a modest rebound in 2024–25.

What the Data Tells Us
Three structural patterns visible in 6 years of MSCS ACT data
📉
Scores Never Recovered Post-COVID
The 2020–21 score drop (17.3 → 16.6) was expected. What's notable is that 2022–23, 2023–24, and 2024–25 all plateau at 16.4 — signaling a structural floor, not a temporary dip.
Source: TDOE Data Downloads
📚
English Scores Drive the Gap
English dropped from 16.5 to 15.0 — the steepest subject decline. In 2024–25, English is the lowest-scoring subject across all four tested areas, consistent with national reading proficiency trends.
Source: TDOE District ACT Files
📈
More Students Testing Each Year
MSCS tested 6,579 students in 2024–25 — 465 more than in 2019–20. Growing participation with declining scores means the gap is real, not an artifact of more lower-scoring students taking the test.
Source: TDOE Data Downloads
